My Town

Walpole, MA

I grew up, and spend most of my time, in the town of Walpole, Massachusetts.
Walpole is a charming town nestled in Norfolk County, Massachusetts, roughly 18 miles south of Boston's bustling center. Founded in 1659, it boasts a rich history, evident in its well-preserved colonial buildings and vibrant sense of community.

Downtown Walpole MA, heading south on Main Street.

The town offers a delightful blend of small-town character and suburban convenience. Walpole Pond, a scenic centerpiece, beckons nature lovers with its walking trails and opportunities for boating and fishing.

Downtown.

History buffs will appreciate the Walpole Historical Society and its collection of artifacts that tell the story of the town's evolution. The Society's headquarters, the historic Aaron Guild House, is an architectural gem dating back to the 18th century.

The former Walpole Town Hall.

For a taste of the local culture, explore the shops and restaurants lining Main Street. From cozy cafes to trendy boutiques, there's something for everyone. Outdoor enthusiasts can explore the scenic trails of the Eleanor Roosevelt Reservation, offering a respite from the urban buzz.
Walpole captures the essence of New England living, offering a peaceful retreat with a strong sense of community, all within easy reach of the excitement of Boston.
